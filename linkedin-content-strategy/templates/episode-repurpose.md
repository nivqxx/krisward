# Episode Repurpose Template

## The Rule

A podcast episode is source material, NOT the content itself. The episode provides the insight — your job is to turn that insight into a LinkedIn post that stands on its own.

**NEVER** post: "New episode! [Title] with [Guest Name]! Link in comments."
That format is Kris's worst-performing content type.

## Step-by-Step Repurpose Process

### Step 1: Extract the Gold

Listen/read the episode and pull out:
- [ ] The single strongest insight or reframe
- [ ] Any concrete client story or example
- [ ] A contrarian take that challenges common advice
- [ ] A metaphor or analogy Kris used naturally
- [ ] Any named framework (Super Toolkits, WIN Team, 60/40 Formula, etc.)

### Step 2: Map to a Buyer House

Ask: who does this insight serve?

| If the insight is about... | It maps to... |
|---|---|
| Being overwhelmed, bottlenecked, working too many hours | House 1: Drowning Achiever |
| Hiring failures, delegation gone wrong, took the work back | House 2: Burned-on-Hiring |
| Both / identity-level ("your business owns you") | Cross-cut / Identity layer |

### Step 3: Pick the Awareness Level

| Level | What the post does |
|---|---|
| Problem-unaware | Makes them feel the wound they've normalized. Pure recognition, no pitch. |
| Problem-aware | Names the system that caused it. "It's not you, it's the architecture." |
| Solution-aware | Shows what building the architecture looks like (Super Toolkits, WIN Team). |
| Product-aware | Named IP: WIN Team, 60/40 Formula, Business Architecture Method. |
| Most-aware | Soft bridge to a real conversation. Use sparingly, never stack. |

### Step 4: Draft the LinkedIn Post

Use the `linkedin-post.md` template. Key rules for episode repurposing:

1. Open on the LISTENER'S problem in Kris's own voice
2. The episode insight becomes THE FRAME (middle of the post)
3. Name the guest in line 3 or the first comment — never the hook
4. If there's a great guest quote, paraphrase it in Kris's voice or attribute it casually ("My guest [Name] put it perfectly...")
5. End with a genuine question, not "go listen to the episode"

### Step 5: Build the Full Bundle

For each repurposed episode, produce:

```
LINKEDIN POST
- Main post (150-400 words)
- 2-3 alternate hooks
- First comment (guest credit + soft CTA + episode link here)
- 3 image directions

EMAIL/NEWSLETTER VERSION
- Warmer, 1:1 register
- More depth than the post
- Soft bridge at the end

PODCAST/BLOG ANGLE
- A talking point or direction for Kris's own show
- Not a full script — just the angle
```

### Step 6: Score It

Run through the scoring rubric (`checklists/scoring-rubric.md`). Must hit 95+, zero hard-fail breaches.

## Example: Episode → LinkedIn Post

**Episode topic:** Guest Karen Grill on building repeatable processes

**Extracted gold:** "Most founders think the problem is finding good people. The real problem is they have no system for those people to plug into."

**Buyer house:** House 2 (Burned-on-Hiring) + House 1 (Drowning Achiever)

**Draft:**
```
I used to blame my team when things fell through the cracks.

Then I realized something uncomfortable:
They didn't have cracks to fall through.
They had no floor.

No system. No framework. No architecture.
Just me, explaining the same thing
for the fourth time this week.

Here's what changed everything:
I stopped trying to find better people.
I started building better systems for the people I had.

Super Toolkits. Living frameworks that anyone can follow
without a 45-minute explanation from me every time.

The shift wasn't "hire smarter."
It was "build the floor first."

What's the one process in your business
that still lives entirely in your head?

---
First comment: Had a great conversation with Karen Grill
about this exact shift. If you're stuck in the
"it's faster if I just do it myself" loop,
DM me "systems" — happy to share what worked.
```
