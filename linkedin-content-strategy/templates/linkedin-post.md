# LinkedIn Post Recipe

## Structure

Every LinkedIn post follows this shape:

1. **Hook** (line 1-2): Stop the scroll. Lead with a first-person confession, a contrarian take, or a concrete everyday analogy. NEVER lead with a podcast promo title.
2. **Recognition** (lines 3-6): Paint the reader's situation so precisely they feel seen. Use "you" as the protagonist.
3. **The turn** (line 7-9): Name the system/architecture that caused their pain. Blade on the system, never the reader.
4. **The frame** (lines 10-14): Introduce Kris's angle — the framework, the reframe, the "what actually works." Use named IP when it fits naturally.
5. **The close** (last 1-2 lines): End on a genuine-curiosity question (60% more comments than declarative closers) OR a soft bridge to a real conversation.

## Length

150-400 words. Let her run long — the data says her longer posts outperform. One idea per post. Heavy whitespace. One idea per line.

## Hook Types (ranked by performance)

1. **First-person confession** — highest performing. "I used to think delegation was the answer. Then I hired two people and my workload doubled."
2. **Contrarian reframe** — "Delegation is a lateral move." / "You don't have a time problem."
3. **Analogy-first** — "You wouldn't train for a marathon by running harder every day."
4. **Recognition opener** — "You've got 47 tabs open, three fires to put out, and you skipped lunch again."
5. **NEVER: Podcast promo** — "Title! with [Guest Name]" is the worst performing format (median 11 vs 15 baseline). If referencing an episode, open on the LISTENER'S problem in Kris's voice. Name the guest in line 3 or the first comment.

## Alternate Hooks

Draft 2-3 alternate hooks for every post. Kris picks.

## First Comment

Write one first comment per post. This is where you can:
- Add context or a mini-story
- Name the podcast guest (if episode-based)
- Drop a soft CTA ("DM me 'systems' if this hit home")
- Link to the episode/resource

## Image Direction

3 ranked options per post. Rules:
- Real, unstaged artefacts only (whiteboard, notebook, post-it, book margin)
- Kris's handwriting on surfaces is the signature device
- NEVER use AI-generated images or polished brand graphics
- Flat design, no shadows/glow/glassmorphism
- Yellow Gold (#FEE16E) is the signature field color
- Brand Red (#EB001E) is the ONE hot accent — use sparingly

## Example Post Shape

```
[HOOK — first-person confession or analogy]

[RECOGNITION — paint their day, their feeling, their "I'll deal with it later"]

[THE TURN — name the system that did this to them]

[THE FRAME — Kris's reframe, the framework, what actually works]

[THE CLOSE — genuine question or soft bridge]
```
