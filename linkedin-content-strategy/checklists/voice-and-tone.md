# Voice and Tone — Kris Ward

## The Register

- **Second-person dominant.** "You" is the protagonist. "I" only carries proof, then pivot back. Never an "I" humble-brag opener.
- **Clear, not direct.** She corrects "direct" to "clear" on purpose. Clarity signals she is invested in you, not cold. Never soft/hedged, never cold/harsh.
- **Feel target: 6-7.** Looser and warmer than her over-polished LinkedIn. Write it the way she'd say it on a podcast, then tighten only slightly.
- **Attitude target: 4-5.** Conversational, not corporate.

## The Archetype Mix

- **Frameworker 35%** — Name a framework, show the architecture
- **Opinionator 30%** — Take a contrarian stance, own a position
- **Storyteller 25%** — Prove it with a lived or client story
- **Remaining 10%** — Recognition/empathy weave

## Analogy-First Rule

Open teaching points with a concrete everyday image, then apply to business.

**Primary analogy domains (in order):**
1. Fitness / diet
2. The body
3. Cooking / baking
4. Sports / outdoors

Preserve her metaphors VERBATIM when you find them:
- Paddleboard pinhole
- Roast-beef dinner
- Rust under the hood
- Deflating balloon
- Running shoes "for running"

## Humour

- Structural humour: small relatable situation fast-forwarded to the most absurd visual consequence, delivered deadpan
- Never open on a joke
- Never pre-script jokes
- Mine real disaster-anecdotes from her stories

## Intensifiers (instead of profanity)

- "Ridiculous"
- A hard short sentence
- Sparing ALL-CAPS on ONE load-bearing word
- No profanity in writing, ever

## Owned Words (never thesaurus-vary these)

clear, clarity, systems, architecture, Super Toolkits, WIN Team, leadership/leader, invested, ridiculous, win, recovering rushaholic, "your business should support your life"

## Anti-Patterns

- Never valorize the grind or hustle
- Never position Kris as the hero — the reader's transformation is the story
- Never use an "I had a problem, now I make success" narrative
- Never trauma-dump or use personal grief as content
- Never use vague sweeping claims or big dramatic claims without substance
- Never use bro-marketing or algorithm-optimized small-talk questions
- The over-polished Feel-8 register IS the failure mode — if it reads like a brand or like AI, it's wrong
