Title: AI Won't Replace Your VA, But It Will Expose Something

Subtitle: The question every business owner is quietly asking in 2026, and the honest answer most won't tell you.


A business owner asked me this last week…
half hoping I'd say yes…
so she could skip the hire:

"Kris… do I even need a VA anymore?
ChatGPT does it in nine seconds."

I didn't say yes.

Here's the honest answer…
and it surprises almost everyone:

AI will not replace your virtual assistant.

It will replace the VA…
who refuses to use AI.
And it will absolutely replace you…
trying to do everything alone.

Let me explain.
Because this changes…
how you should spend every dollar…
and every hour this year.


What AI is genuinely great at

It's fast, tireless, and cheap.
Hand it the busywork and never look back:

→ First drafts. Emails, outlines, captions
→ Summaries. A 40 minute call turned into three bullets
→ Research. Options and starting points in seconds
→ Formatting. Cleaning up the messy stuff

If a task is fast, repeatable…
and doesn't need someone who knows your world…
that's AI's job now.

Doing it yourself…
is just expensive stubbornness.


Where the machine quietly falls apart

→ It doesn't own outcomes. It produces and walks away.
→ It doesn't read the room. The annoyed client, the deadline that quietly moved.
→ It doesn't do the thing. Make the call, handle the exception, move the project while you sleep.
→ It doesn't get better at your business. It starts from zero every time, unless a person trained it. That person is your VA.

That's not a small gap.
That's the whole game.


Here's what AI actually exposed

For years, some owners hid behind…
"I can't afford help"…
or "no one does it like me."

AI just called the bluff.

Because now the busywork excuse is gone.
AI handles 60 to 70% of it.

What's left is the work…
that needs a human who owns it.
And that was always the real hire.

This is why I don't build teams of task doers.

I build Win Teams.
People who own their zone…
so the business runs…
without you as the bottleneck.

AI didn't make that idea old.
It made it more powerful.

One great team member…
with the right tools…
now carries what used to take three.

I watched an owner go from drowning…
in her business every waking hour…
sure no one could do it like her…
to 25 hours a week back…
in her first month.

Not because a robot replaced her.
Because a person, armed with AI…
finally owned the work…
she'd been white knuckling alone.

So before you cancel the hire, ask one question:

Do you want a faster tool…
or your time back?

If you just need speed…
start with AI today.
It's the cheapest hire you'll ever make.

If the business stops when you stop…
no tool fixes that.
You need a person who owns outcomes…
using AI to move faster.

Most owners need both.
In that order.
